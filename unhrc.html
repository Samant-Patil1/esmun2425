About UNHRC

The UN Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body responsible for promoting and protecting human rights around the globe. It addresses human rights violations and makes recommendations on human rights issues.

Established in 2006, the Council replaced the UN Commission on Human Rights and consists of 47 member states elected by the UN General Assembly. The Council conducts Universal Periodic Reviews, addresses urgent human rights situations, and develops international human rights standards.

In ESMUN's UNHRC simulation, delegates will tackle contemporary human rights challenges including refugee protection, gender equality, freedom of expression, minority rights, and accountability for human rights violations. The committee emphasizes collaborative problem-solving and constructive dialogue.

Key Features

  • Universal Coverage: Address human rights in all UN member states
  • Special Procedures: Independent experts and working groups
  • Constructive Dialogue: Cooperative approach to human rights
  • Civil Society: Strong NGO participation and input
